        Why Concentration Breaks Down

        Common causes: too many open tasks in your mind, phone/internet distractions, starting without a clear next step, perfectionism, mental fatigue, poor sleep or exercise, constant task-switching, and work that feels too big. The fix in every case is the same: reduce mental load and make focusing the path of least resistance.
        Two-Minute Rule: if something takes under two minutes, do it now — replying, renaming a file, approving a request.
        Batch similar tasks: 90 min coding, 30 min email, 30 min calls, 90 min coding — not constant switching between the four.
        Stop at 80%. Take breaks before your brain crashes, not after.
        Sleep and exercise improve concentration more reliably than any productivity hack: 7–8 hours, consistent bed/wake time, 20–30 minutes of movement daily.
        If poor concentration is persistent for months, spans many areas of life, or comes with significant restlessness, forgetfulness, or impulsivity, it's worth discussing with a qualified healthcare professional — conditions like chronic stress, anxiety, sleep disorders, or ADHD affect focus, and an evaluation can point to the right support.
